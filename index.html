About & Focus Areas

Where the “mechanical engineer” and the “ML person” agree to cooperate.

I’m a B.Tech Mechanical Engineering student at IIT Delhi with a minor in Robotics. My work so far sits on a spectrum: from FEM-based heat transfer and Hyperloop hardware, to agentic RAG systems, optimization models and financial ML.

I enjoy problems where you can’t fake understanding: modelling conduction plates, routing grain across states, or learning navigation policies from raw trajectories. That usually means digging into theory first and then building systems that are repeatable, testable and debuggable.

Long term, I want to work on autonomous physical systems and decision engines — the stuff that moves real-world mass or real-world money, ideally both.

Mechanical Core Heat transfer, solid mechanics and manufacturing, backed by CAD/FEA and lab work.
Data Science & ML From classic ML to deep models for forecasting, anomaly detection and representation learning.
Autonomous Systems Navigation, imitation learning and control — getting robots to behave intelligently in the loop.
Financial Maths Time-series, risk signals and ML on markets; more interested in structure than hype.

Deep Learning Surrogate for 2D Heat Conduction

Generated thousands of FEM simulations for 2D conduction plates under varying boundary conditions, then trained CNN surrogates to predict steady-state temperature fields with < 3% error vs FEM, at ~25× faster inference.
